Sommelier

A Scalable Web-Speech Pre-Processing Pipeline for Full-Duplex Speech Language Models

Paper arXiv Code

Overview of the Sommelier pipeline for curating podcast-style, multi-turn conversational speech suitable for full-duplex training.

Introduction

A key frontier in human–AI speech interaction is full-duplex dialogue, where listening and speaking occur simultaneously, enabling natural turn-taking, interruptions, and real-time backchanneling. This capability is particularly important in industry, where commercial voice assistants must deliver fluid, human-like interaction to meet user expectations.

Despite early progress by systems like Moshi, advancement remains constrained by a scarcity of high-quality conversational data. While Moshi leverages millions of hours of unsupervised audio for pre-training, these recordings are predominantly single-stream and offer limited supervision for overlapping speech, leaving overlap robustness dependent on relatively small high-fidelity resources like Fisher, unlikely to suffice for the thousands of hours of diverse SFT data required by modern speech models.

Building datasets for full-duplex interaction is particularly challenging because real-world dialogue contains overlapping speech, background noise, and backchanneling that complicate reliable curation. Beyond these local acoustic challenges, it is critical to ensure that speech streams retain their conversational context, enabling the model to engage in contextually coherent dialogue. Additionally, maintaining a reasonable RTF (Real-Time Factor) per audio sample is critical in real-world industry settings for processing large-scale datasets.

To address these challenges, we propose an open, robust, and scalable web-speech pre-processing pipeline designed for full-duplex SLMs.

Contributions

  • Scalable Pipeline for Full-Duplex SLMs: We release a scalable pipeline for curating podcast-style, multi-turn conversational speech suitable for full-duplex training, helping alleviate the community-wide data scarcity.
  • High-Fidelity Overlap Processing: We provide a detailed processing strategy that explicitly handles overlaps via rigorous diarization analysis and reduces ASR hallucinations using paralleled model ensembling and n-gram filtering.
  • Full-Duplex Fine-Tuning and Data Requirement Insights: We validate our pipeline by fine-tuning the full-duplex model Moshi on Sommelier-processed speech and analyze practical data requirements for stable full-duplex training.
